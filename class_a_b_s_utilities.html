Calculates the DM of a series of cash flows.

  • Dte The list of dates at which there is a flow
  • Flws The list of cash flows
  • BaseRate The base rate over which to calculate the margin
  • Daycount The daycount convention, put 360 for Actual/360
  • guess The starting guess for the IRR (usually 0.05)
  • precision The order of magnitude of the largest error. 0 will make it precise to the unity, 2 will make it precise to the cent (the higher the precision the slower the function). Usually 3.

This function calculates the discount margin over a base rate of a series of cash flows happening on specific dates.
When Flws contains more than negative number then there can be multiple roots and changing Guess may change the result of the function.

Calculates the payment for a loan based on constant payments and a constant interest rate.

  • Inter The interest rate for the loan in scalar form (5%=0.05)
  • Periods The total number of payments for the loan
  • PresentValue Is the present value, or the total amount that a series of future payments is worth now; also known as the principal.
    Warning
    Inter must be referring to the same time interval between two payments.
    To convert an annual rate \( r \) for a loan that pays interest \( p \) times a year apply the formula Inter \(= \left((1+r)^\frac{p}{12} \right)-1 \)
